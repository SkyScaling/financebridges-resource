You're In Better Shape Than Most.

Based on your answers, our income protection check isn't the right fit for you right now, and that's usually a good sign. It means you've already got some safety net. Here's how to make sure it actually holds up if you ever need it.

What To Check On Now
Simple, free things worth doing to make sure your income is really protected.

Know What It Actually Pays

If you have coverage through work or a policy, check what it really replaces. A lot of plans only cover a fraction of your income, or stop after a set number of months.

Read The Fine Print

Group plans often define "disability" narrowly. Find out whether yours pays if you can't do your job, or only if you can't do any job. It's a big difference.

Watch The Gap

If your side income ever becomes your main income, your safety net changes overnight. That's the moment your work coverage stops carrying you, and the moment to come back here.

Build Your Own Buffer

Aim for 3 to 6 months of expenses saved. It's the simplest income protection there is, it's free, and it's yours no matter what.
